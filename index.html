Muted resale activity in the landed resale market in October

According to the caveat data from URA Realis, less than a hundred transactions of resale landed homes took place in October 2023, amassing a total transaction value of $508 million. This shows a slight decline from the sales performance in September 2023, which saw a total of 114 deals with a combined value of $608 million. The majority of these transactions were lucrative, upon examining each transaction and its returns. The top ten transactions, ranked by gross profit, were spread out across Singapore, in locales such as Bukit Timah, Novena, and Bedok. These top ten deals in landed home transactions recorded profits in the range of roughly $4.3 million to $11.5 million.

Despite the current economic instability and high-interest rates, prospective homeowners continue to deliberate over the increasing costs of landed properties. Unyielding sales prices set by sellers have subsequently resulted in a slowdown in sales. There has been a noticeable increase in the sale of high-cost landed homes in October compared to the previous month. According to data from URA Realis caveats, approximately 38.5% of the resale landed homes sold in October were priced at $5 million and above - a slight increase from about 36% in September. On the other hand, about 60.4% of resale landed homes sold in October were priced below $5 million, which is somewhat lower than the 64% seen in the previous month.

The profit gained from each unit's resale transaction is determined by comparing the price to its last recorded transaction. The reported gains, however, are gross figures - they haven't been adjusted for relevant expenses such as seller’s stamp duties, interest payments, taxes, and other divestment costs.

The Annualised gain signifies the cumulative yearly rate of return that displays the rate of return over the duration from the last agreed upon transaction to the point of resale, presented in yearly percentage terms. Essentially, this can be calculated using the formula: [(current resale value)/(original purchase price)] to the power of time frame in years minus 1.

Top landed transaction with highest gains (overall)

The month's highest transaction was for a GCB in the CCR, situated in Capitol Park at Chee Hoon Avenue, one of Singapore's GCB precincts (District 11). The property was purchased for a whopping $18.5 million, up by an incredible $11.5 million from its previously lodged caveat in January 2010. This resulted in a notable annualised profit of 7.4%. The GCB boasts a huge land area of over 11,000 sq ft, and the acquisition price reflects a per-square-foot rate of $1,678 on the land area. This property is conveniently located a short walk from the Botanic Gardens MRT interchange, on the Circle Line and Downtown Line, providing access to the city in less than 20 minutes. The iconic Singapore Botanic Gardens are also located nearby.

  

Top landed transaction with highest gains (Core Central Region)

In the Tanglin planning area (District 10), a detached corner house in Robin Lane clinched the second highest transaction for landed properties in the CCR. Touted at $13.6 million, the property brought about a projected profit of $10.9 million, translating to an annualised return of about 8.8% each year from its last documented transaction in June 2004. The holding period lasted for almost two decades. The property’s prime location near the Stevens MRT station, enables quick transportation to the city within 15 minutes on the Downtown Line. Notably, Raffles Town Club, Orchard Road shopping district, and the Singapore Botanic Gardens are all readily accessible from this location. Additionally, prestigious institutions namely St Joseph’s Institution and Singapore Chinese Girls’ School, are also within a walkable distance from the property.

The third highest transaction in the Core Central Region (CCR) for landed properties was a semi-detached house in the Novena locality (District 11), located on Dunearn Road. The sale price was slightly over $9 million, the most recent clause being filed in July 2005. There has been a surge of $7.26 million from the last recorded price, resulting in an annualised profit of 9.2% across an 18-year span. The property is conveniently located within walking distance of the Tan Kah Kee MRT station on the Downtown Line, connecting residents to the city centre in less than 25 minutes. Additionally, Hwa Chong Institution is easily accessible by foot from the property, that is also in close proximity to National Junior College, Raffles Girls’ School, and Nanyang Girls’ School.

 

Top landed transaction with highest gains (Rest of Central Region)

Two landed transactions in the Rest of Central Region (RCR) made it to the top ten list in October. The first was the sale of an independent residence situated along Jalan Pemimpin in the Thomson Rise landed estate, part of Bishan town (District 20). This property boasts a prime location accessible by foot from the Bishan transportation hub, which comprises the Bishan MRT interchange on the North-South line and Circle Line, as well as the Bishan Bus Interchange. The house fetched a price of $10.7 million and the last caveat was lodged in October 2007. The latest sale price marks an increase of $6.97 million from the last known caveated price, translating into an average yearly gain of 6.9% over a 16-year period.

The second highest-ranking city fringe transaction was a detached house in the Bukit Timah area (District 21), located on Hua Guan Avenue. This property sold for over $13 million, its last caveat filed in March 2020. This price showed an increase of $4.14 million from the previous caveat value, resulting in an annual profit of 11.4% in less than four years. Its location is highly desirable, being just a short walk from the King Albert Park MRT station on the Downtown Line, facilitating a less than 30 minutes commute to the city centre. Notably, the house is close to the Methodist Girls’ School, the Sime Darby Centre, and the recently opened railway corridor park connector.

Top landed transaction with highest gains (Outside Central Region)

In October, the most lucrative transaction in the suburbs was a corner detached house on Frankel Avenue in Bedok (District 15) that sold for $10.3 million. This was a considerable increase from the last recorded price of $2.8 million back in March 2007, translating into an annual rise in profit of 8.2% over more than 16 years. The property is a two-storey detached house nestled within the Frankel landed estate. It is conveniently situated within a 10-minute walk from the Kembangan MRT station on the East-West Line and just a quick drive from the various services in Bedok town, including the Bedok Mall, an integrated transport hub, hawker centre, and a polyclinic.

A significant suburban landed sale also noted within the top 10 rankings was the purchase of a semi-detached dwelling at Burnfoot Terrace, located inside the Frankel landed estate in District 15 (Bedok). The property, which is freehold, was purchased for a sum of $8.8 million, a $4.6 million increase from its last recorded caveat back in June 2022. The annualised profit for this sale tallied close to 80.7%, following a short holding tenure of slightly over a year. The dwelling in question is currently undergoing a redevelopment into a brand new 2-storey semi-detached property. Notably, there has been a surge of interest amongst potential investors for properties within the East Coast-Bedok region, due to the area's improving accessibility, wide range of commercial facilities, and a host of acclaimed eateries lining East Coast Road.
