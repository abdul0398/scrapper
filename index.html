As the year draws to a close, it's a time to reflect on past events and plan for future ones. Reflecting on 2023, it's clear that it has been an eventful period for the residential property market. This year was packed with policy announcements that had a significant effect on both private and public housing sectors.

From increasing additional buyer’s stamp duty (ABSD) rates, to escalating marginal buyer’s stamp duty rates for more expensive properties, to a wide range of modifications made to public housing policies, like the more stringent non-selection regulation for build-to-order (BTO) flats, and the reclassification of flats beginning in the latter half of 2024, among other things. For those who are not familiar with these, it may seem overwhelming.

In light of the cooling measures implemented in April 2023, several proposed policy changes, and persistent interest rates, some potential purchasers have chosen to take a pause to assess their alternatives. However, for those who still have a keen interest in purchasing a new residence or investment real estate, 2024 will offer some invigorating prospects. Here's a list of potential new projects that may enter the market during the initial half of 2024.

Among the various projects in the pipeline, a couple stand out as potential showcase projects due to their distinctive features or as pioneers in emerging districts. An ideal example of this is the Skywaters Residences (previously known as AXA Tower) located on Anson Road. Upon completion, it is set to become Singapore's highest building, usurping The Guoco Tower that currently holds the record at 283.7m. Equally significant is the project situated at Marina Gardens Lane, due to its status as the first site sale within the new Marina South district. Both Skywaters Residences and the Marina Gardens Lane project are situated within a short walking distance of an MRT station; specifically Tanjong Pagar station and the upcoming Marina South station respectively. Interestingly, a notable portion of the soon to be released projects are conveniently located less than a ten-minute walk from an MRT station.

Buyers who rank MRT accessibility highly should look into the following properties: The ex-Meyer Park site, just a 7-minute walk to the future Katong Park MRT station; A property at Bukit Timah Road & Duke’s Road, which is 7 minutes away from the Botanic Gardens MRT interchange station; The Hill @ One North, a mere 6 minutes from Buona Vista MRT interchange station; The former La Ville's site, a short 6-minute walk from the upcoming Katong Park MRT station; The old Peace Centre site, located 6 minutes away from Bencoolen MRT station; The project at Mount Emily, just 6 minutes from Little Indian MRT station; Lentoria, a 5-minute stroll from Lentor MRT station; The Lentor Gardens property, ideally situated 5 minutes from Lentor MRT station; Hillhaven which is 7 minutes from Hillview MRT station and finally The Arcady @ Boon Keng, a 7-minute distance from Boon Keng MRT station.

The following development projects are expected to be in close proximity to MRT stations: The Bukit Timah Link project is only 3 minutes away from Beauty World MRT station; the Marina View Residences are a mere 2 minutes from Shenton Way MRT station; the Newport Residences will be 5 minutes away from the forthcoming Prince Edward Road MRT station; the former Bagnall Court site will be a 1-minute walk away from the upcoming Sungei Bedok MRT station; the former Chuan Park project will be 1 minute from Lorong Chuan MRT station; the Marina Gardens Lane site will be 1 minute away from the future Marina South MRT station; and the Skywaters Residences are set to be 1 minute away from Tanjong Pagar MRT station.

According to the Home Buyers' sentiment survey 2023 by PropNex, 40% of respondents highlighted the importance of having their residential property near transport nodes. Considering the considerable number of imminent property launches near an MRT station, it is predicted that there could be significant buying interest, particularly if the units are priced appropriately.

In 2024, potential purchasers looking for mixed-use projects will have several options. Both The Hill @ One North and the Marina Gardens Lane project are designated for residential use, with commercial spaces on the ground floor. Similarly, Newport Residences (formerly the Fuji Xerox Tower) and Skywaters Residences in the city centre will feature commercial pods. Mixed-use developments are often more appealing to buyers, given their convenience and potential for increased capital appreciation in the future.

Although city properties appear to offer all the necessary amenities, they lack closeness to educational institutions. Looking at the new development launches anticipated in the first half of 2024, a number of them are located near a good array of schools. The Jalan Tembusu development, for instance, boasts of over 10 schools in its immediate vicinity. Similarly, the earlier East Court location is nothing short of eight primary schools in close proximity, among which - CHIJ (Katong) Primary, Haig Girls’ School, Tanjong Katong Primary, and Tao Nan School - fall within 1-km radius of the project site. Also, the former La Ville location within District 15 is just a quick walk away from Dunman High School.

In Singapore, the geographical location of a home in relation to a school impacts admission precedence for Primary 1 enrolment. Certain investors also hold the conviction that the closeness to desirable schools can sway property values.

The two projects situated in the Lentor Hills estate are in proximity to CHIJ St. Nicholas Girls’ School. The project located on Bukit Timah Road & Duke’s Road can be found within 1 km of both Nanyang Primary and Raffles Girls’ Primary. The Hill @ One North project is conveniently located near Fairfield Methodist Primary, New Town Primary, and Henry Park Primary schools.

The Bukit Timah Link project is conveniently situated close to Pei Hwa Presbyterian Primary and Ngee Ann Polytechnic. Conversely, the project located on Mount Emily Road is anticipated to be near future St. Margaret’s Primary and other art-focused educational institutions like LaSalle College of The Arts, Nanyang Academy of Fine Arts, and School of the Arts.

If nature appeals to you, a couple of projects might just tick the boxes. Hillhaven, located in Hillview Rise, and the soon to be launched project at Bukit Timah Link are a mere walk away from the Bukit Timah Nature Reserve, which is where Singapore's tallest hill, Bukit Timah Hill, can be found. For those who value direct access to ample green spaces, the new development Sora on Yuan Ching Road is practically unbeatable with the expansive Jurong Lake Gardens just next door.

If you desire a residence that is nearer to the heart of the city but still provides an easy access to natural environments, the following developments may suit your needs. For example, the upcoming project located at the Bukit Timah Road & Duke’s Road is conveniently located within walking distances of the Singapore Botanic Gardens, the nation's first UNESCO Heritage Site. Similarly, Marina View Residences and the development in Marina Gardens Lane are located incredibly close to a major hotspot of Singapore, the Gardens by the Bay.

Considering all factors, there are superb choices scheduled for the first half of 2024. These include projects that are in close proximity to nature, key transportation nodes, schools and amenities, ensuring convenient accessibility.
